How to operate TIME

A quick-start field guide for new observers and visiting students. Always follow site-specific safety policies and your shift lead’s direction.

Go to observing checklist Back to outreach site

Session prep

  • Verify weather, precipitable water vapor (PWV), and wind limits
  • Confirm cryostat base temperature and vacuum levels
  • Sync telescope ephemerides and time servers
  • Load nightly scan script templates and calibrators

Observing run

Standard observing checklist

Before sunset

  1. Inspect telescope drives and weather stow interlocks.
  2. Confirm cryostat temperatures are stable and dT is within spec.
  3. Check KID tuning file is current; reload if the sky background changed.
  4. Run quick pointing check on a bright quasar; update offsets.

Startup & calibration

  1. Warm up readout chain; verify LO lock and synth references.
  2. Perform noise diode or sky-dip to track atmospheric opacity.
  3. Take a short calibrator scan (planet or bright line source).
  4. Save metadata: date, operator, weather, tuning version, scan script.

During scans

  1. Monitor telescope tracking errors and wind shake.
  2. Watch detector load curves and gain; retune if gain drifts.
  3. Log anomalies (dropouts, RFI spikes, clouds) with UTC timestamps.
  4. Alternate science scans with short calibrator hits every 60–90 min.

Shutdown

  1. Stow telescope if winds exceed limits or at end of shift.
  2. Stop data acquisition; back up raw data to on-site storage.
  3. Export run summary: pointing offsets, opacity, system temperature.
  4. Leave readout warm or cold per shift-lead instructions.

Cryogenic system

Cryostat care and cooldown

Cooldown outline

  1. Pump to high vacuum (< 1e-5 mbar) with turbopump; confirm leak checks.
  2. Start pulse-tube cooler; monitor temperature plateaus and vibration.
  3. Engage sub-K stage (e.g., sorption or dilution) once 4 K is stable.
  4. Record base temperatures of each stage and detector array.

Health checks

  • Pressure rise < 5e-6 mbar/hr after isolation indicates tight seals.
  • Vibration sensors within limits to avoid pointing jitter.
  • Temperature drifts < 10 mK over 10 minutes before observing.
  • Spare getters and O-rings stocked before long campaigns.

Spectrometers

Grating spectrometer quick notes

Alignment

  • Verify feedhorn and slit alignment with near-field source before run.
  • Check grating angle encoders; ensure home position is registered.
  • Run a spectral line source to map channel spacing and band edges.

Throughput tips

  • Keep optics covers closed until the dome is dust-free.
  • Re-seat and torque waveguide connections during maintenance.
  • Log any polarization-dependent response when using calibrators.

Detectors

TES readout checklist

Tuning

  • Load bias map; flag unstable pixels before on-sky scans.
  • Set TES bias to keep detectors at the superconducting transition.
  • Retune after large PWV changes or sunrise heating.

Data quality

  • Track system temperature ($T_\mathrm{sys}$) and gain stability.
  • Remove cosmic-ray spikes and radio-frequency interference in QA.
  • Cross-check detector noise PSDs against reference curves.

Safety & etiquette

Stay safe on site

People first

  • Follow ARO site safety briefings; log entries and exits.
  • Use harnesses and two-person rule for elevated platforms.
  • Wear hearing protection near compressors and pumps.

Equipment care

  • Lock out power before servicing drives or cryo hardware.
  • Use clean-room gloves for optics; keep desiccant dry packs fresh.
  • Do not leave cryostat warm unattended; schedule with leads.
